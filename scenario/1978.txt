Tall buildings to shade smaller, building positioned so that the east and west façades have smallest area (low solar gains)
Tall buildings positioned toward the city (away from water front) to enable maximum view of the water and reduce obstruction of ocean breezes
China Towns and luxurious hotels near trade hubs (assumed red) for ease of access and trade
Mixing of classes and with social housing positioned next to luxury hotels
Strategic mixing of building typology – low density next to high density to allow for greater spacing -between towers  - allowing better ventilation, less radiative exchange and more light for vegetation (passive cooling techniques) 
Entire precinct connected via radiated green corridors to encourage transit via non-automotive routes and to allow ease of access.
Large park precincts along water front to encourage the public to actively participate.
